## 📄 FULL CASE STUDY

### HEADLINE
TalentBridge Kenya Reduced Hiring Cycle by 62% — Without Increasing Headcount

---

### CHALLENGE

TalentBridge Kenya is a staffing and recruitment business serving mid-market clients across Nairobi and Mombasa. With a five-person HR team managing 15–20 active roles at any given time, the business was structurally over-capacity.

Every hire followed the same manual sequence: applications arrived by email, were reviewed one by one, shortlists were built in spreadsheets, and interview scheduling required back-and-forth coordination between candidates and hiring managers. On average, the process consumed 18–22 hours of team time per role and stretched the total hiring cycle to 21 days.

The cost was measurable: roles stayed unfilled longer, clients escalated, and the HR team had no bandwidth to improve candidate quality or sourcing strategy. Growth was stalled by process, not by demand.

---

### SOLUTION

SARVAX deployed its HR Agent to take over the full top-of-funnel hiring process.

From go-live, application intake, qualification screening, shortlist generation, and interview scheduling were fully automated. Every candidate received structured, consistent treatment — regardless of volume. The HR team's role shifted from managing the process to reviewing shortlists and making final decisions.

The agent was operational within five business days. No system replacement was required — it integrated directly into TalentBridge's existing candidate communication workflows.

---

### RESULTS

**Hard Benefits:**
- Hiring cycle reduced from 21 days → 8 days (62% faster)
- 18 hours of manual screening and scheduling recovered per active role
- HR team capacity expanded to manage 3× the previous volume of active roles simultaneously
- Equivalent of 1.5 FTE of coordination work automated
- Estimated monthly operational saving: $2,400 in recovered team hours

**Soft Benefits:**
- Consistent screening criteria applied across 100% of applications — no bias from reviewer fatigue
- Zero dropped candidates due to scheduling delays or missed follow-ups
- Real-time pipeline status visible to leadership without manual reporting
- HR team refocused on sourcing quality and client relationships rather than administrative execution
